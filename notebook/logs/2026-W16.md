## What worked

- Learned RX matches perfect-CSI ZF oracle within ±14% across 0–25 dB and beats EPA LS+interp by 1.4–4× across the full range. At 25 dB: 1.23e-3 vs oracle 1.78e-3 (1.45×) vs LS+interp 5.07e-3 (4.1×).
- 834k params is enough; no scaling required.
- On-the-fly data generation keeps the training loop clean; attention over 4200 tokens is the compute bottleneck, not data generation.
- Training pipeline is deterministic under the curriculum — rerun on fresh RunPod pod reproduced step-26000 val BER of 4.333e-2 to four digits.
- HF Hub auto-upload in `train.py` (added after the original pod termination) kept the rerun safe: best.pt, final.pt, history, training_status all on `makarkul/cognitive-rf-E01`.

## What failed or surprised

- **Original "30× below oracle at 25 dB" was statistical noise.** 32 subframes/SNR gave only ~19 error events at 25 dB, well within Poisson uncertainty. 500-subframe re-eval gave the real margin: 1.45×. The underlying model is the same — only the eval precision changed. E04 (planned as "statistical tightening") was folded into this re-eval and is complete.
- Bump in learned BER at 17.5 dB disappeared in the tighter eval — confirming it was MC noise, not a training artefact.
- First eval attempt hit a `PermissionError` on the PDF output because the previous PDF was open in a viewer. Use `ber_learned_v2.pdf` as a workaround.
- Original RunPod pod was terminated mid-session and the checkpoint was lost. Triggered the HF Hub auto-upload refactor; no data lost on the re-run.
